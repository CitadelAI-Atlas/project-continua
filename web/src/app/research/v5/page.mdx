# v5: raw-audio protocol

**Period:** May 2026
**Status:** round 1 complete, protocol established as the canonical experimental form

## The step v5 contributed

Earlier rounds analyzed audio with a project-specific analyzer that surfaced
features like "blocks" and "pulses" to the receiver. v5 took the next step:
the receiver gets the raw .wav file and runs its own analysis tools
(numpy, scipy, FFT) deciding for itself what to compute. This is the cleanest
possible test of whether the audio's mathematical structure alone is
sufficient to convey meaning, with no shared interpretive vocabulary.

## Headline finding

The raw-audio protocol works. The most capable receiver derived the
target integer on every COUNT trial. Where receivers gave different
answers, the variance traced to their choice of analysis approach, not
to anything missing from the audio. This made receiver capability,
rather than vocabulary ambiguity, the dominant variable in the work
that followed.

## What this version contributed to the program

- A protocol where receivers analyze raw audio directly. No
  project-specific analyzer in the loop. This is the canonical
  experimental form for any future "can a receiver derive this from the
  audio alone?" question.
- A pre-registered scoring framework (rubric locked before any agent
  ran) that the project now applies uniformly.
- Anonymized, sealed-mapping test design so filenames carry no semantic
  cues.
- An empirical demonstration that the protocol works end-to-end:
  receivers loaded the WAV, ran their own analyses, and produced
  grounded derivations without any tool-execution failures.

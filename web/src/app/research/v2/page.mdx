# v2: 20-primitive chord grammar

**Period:** Early 2026
**Status:** complete; established the math-native encoder and the central problem the project still works on

## The step v2 contributed

v1 used eight convention-based symbols. v2 made two large advances:

1. **Tripled the vocabulary** to twenty primitives organized into a
   three-band chord grammar (bass, midrange, treble), so multiple primitives
   can play simultaneously and be distinguished by frequency band.
2. **Made the encoder math-native.** All primitive frequencies are derived
   from integer ratios and harmonic relationships rather than arbitrary
   assignment. Pitch, rhythm, and chord structure all carry mathematical
   meaning at the encoder side.

The round-trip benchmark (encoder produces audio, decoder reads it back)
revealed an asymmetry: the encoder was math-native, but the decoder still
needed the spec to interpret incoming audio.

## Headline finding

**Math-native encoding does not imply math-native decoding.** This
asymmetry was the project's central problem statement going forward and
shaped every subsequent round: v3 tested whether richer features helped
the decoder (negative result), and v4 reframed the constraint so that
encoding must be receiver-derivable from the audio structure itself.

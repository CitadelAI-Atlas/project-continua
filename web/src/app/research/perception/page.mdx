# Probe 1: Perception study (in progress)

**Period:** May 2026, ongoing
**Status:** design and instruments shipped; recruitment opens Week 4

## What this probe asks

The project rests on a claim it has not yet measured: that humans can
extract more context from structured sound than from text or speech of
the same content. Music carries feeling that lyrics alone do not. A
court reporter writes through chords rather than letters because
simultaneity is the bandwidth. Whether engineered math-native audio can
make that wider channel reachable for everyday communication with
machines is what the project exists to find out.

Probe 1 is the first measurement. It is small on purpose. It compares
math-structured audio against matched plain text and matched
synthesized speech on the same content, with a parallel set of in-depth
interviews on a smaller group. A null result is a real result, and the
next probe&apos;s design depends on what this one finds.

## How the study is structured

Two arms.

### Main study: three-channel matched-trio

Six math-native v4 primitives, each presented in three matched
conditions per listener:

1. **Audio**: the rendered v4 expression as math-native audio.
2. **Text**: the plain-English description of the same content
   shown on screen.
3. **Speech**: the same plain-English description read by a
   synthesized voice.

Eighteen trials per session (six primitives across three conditions),
randomized order under the constraint that no two consecutive trials
share a primitive. After each trial the listener writes a short
free-response, rates the trial on three Likert scales (valence,
intensity, evocativeness), and picks the best-matching structural
description from four options.

Target sample is twelve to fifteen listeners, with a deliberately
recruited subgroup of musicians and tonal-language native speakers
because trained perception is where the channel, if it exists, would
show up most cleanly.

### Interview track: in-depth phenomenology

A separate three-person arm. Sixty-minute recorded conversations on the
audio condition alone, conducted using micro-phenomenological
elicitation. The questions are slow, open-ended, in the participant&apos;s
own words. No pre-defined categories. The interview track surfaces
themes that the structured main study cannot ask about because it does
not know to ask.

## What the study will report

For each primitive in each condition, the analysis reports:

- Whether the audio response surfaces structural content that the text
  and speech responses for the same primitive do not.
- The within-listener Likert differences across the three conditions.
- The count of audio-only content elements per listener.
- Whether the expert subgroup differs from novice listeners by a
  meaningful effect size.
- Emergent themes from the three interview transcripts that appear in
  two or more participants.

The decision gate is pre-registered. If no audio-specific effects
surface anywhere across the main study, and the interview track turns
up no convergent themes specific to the audio condition, the project
suspends new engineering development indefinitely. The threshold is
explicit so the result does not get talked around.

## Why two arms

The single-condition main study is psychophysics: structured prompts,
forced-choice options, Likert scales. It detects measurable behavioral
signal across listeners. The interview track is phenomenology: slow
description in a listener&apos;s own words, no pre-defined categories,
inductive coding. It surfaces what the structured study cannot ask.
Each arm answers a different kind of question; the combination is
stronger than either alone.

## Why these six primitives

The six are the cross-model-stable primitives that cover all distinct
types in the current v4 vocabulary:

- **`COUNT(3)`**: discrete counting.
- **`RATIO(3, 2)`**: simultaneous integer ratio (a perfect fifth).
- **`BECOMES(220, 440)`**: continuous glide, one octave rise.
- **`PERIOD(0.5, COUNT(3))`**: composed periodic structure.
- **`AND(COUNT(4), RATIO(3, 2))`**: superposition of pulse and
  chord.
- **`TRANSFORMATION(220, 440, 330, 880)`**: discrete step
  sequence.

Four longer primitives from the current catalog
(`COUNT(7)`, `RATIO(5, 4)`, `IMPLIES_MULTI`, `FUNCTION_MULTI`) are
deferred to the next probe. They are not abandoned, just too long for a
three-condition session that fits a thirty-minute attention budget.

## Honest framing of what one probe can do

Probe 1 cannot settle the moonshot. It tests sparse atomic primitives
in controlled conditions. It cannot say anything about richer timbral
audio, longer composed sequences, machine-to-human contextual transfer,
or whether the effect (if any) is generic to structured sound or
specific to math-native structure. Those questions belong to later
probes. This probe is the first cheap test whose result determines
whether the probe sequence is worth continuing.

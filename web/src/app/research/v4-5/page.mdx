# v4.4 - v4.5: cross-model methodology

**Period:** May 2026
**Status:** complete; methodology practices established as the project standard

## The step v4.4 / v4.5 contributed

Earlier v4 rounds tested each primitive against a single capable receiver
model. v4.4 expanded this to three models in parallel (Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6,
Haiku 4.5) so that math-native claims could be triangulated across
receiver-capability tiers. v4.5 then upgraded the analyzer to surface gap
hierarchy and cross-block invariants, added the multi-instance NEGATE
formulation, and locked in the pre-registration practice that the project
still uses.

By the end of these two rounds the project had a measured picture of which
primitives are stable across receivers, plus a set of methodology practices
that every subsequent round inherits.

## Headline finding

Cross-model variance is the dominant signal, not vocabulary ambiguity. When
all three Claude-family receivers agree on a primitive's derivation, the
audio is unambiguous. When they disagree, the variance traces to
characteristic per-model analysis priors. This reframed the project: future
encoder work should focus on what survives cross-model agreement, and
future receiver-side work can simply assume the codec is shared.

## State of the vocabulary after v4.5

Seven primitives derive cleanly across all three models tested:

- `COUNT(N)` (the integer N)
- `RATIO(p:q)` (simultaneous frequencies in small-integer ratio)
- `BECOMES` (continuous transformation, frequency glide with timbre evolution)
- `AND` (co-presence of distinct mathematical objects)
- `PERIOD(T)` (periodic repetition with period T)
- `IMPLIES_MULTI` (functional correspondence shown via paired instances)
- `FUNCTION_MULTI` (input-output map shown via labeled examples)

Three additional primitives derive cleanly with the most capable receiver
and remain in the vocabulary as receiver-capability-sensitive:

- `OR` (alternation between alternatives)
- `SEQUENCE` (ordered presentation of elements)
- `EQUAL_MULTI` (computational equivalence between structurally different expressions)

## Methodology practices introduced in this round

These v4.4 / v4.5 practices are now standard for every subsequent round:

1. **Cross-model testing.** Every derivation runs on multiple models in
   parallel. Headline numbers are cross-model.
2. **Pre-registration.** Expected derivations and scoring rubrics are
   committed before any agent runs. No post-hoc adjustment.
3. **Verbatim prompts.** Every prompt sent to a receiver is published
   verbatim in the writeup.
4. **Sealed mappings.** Anonymized stimulus filenames prevent
   filename-based shortcutting.

## What this version contributed to the program

- The cross-model methodology that turns claims about math-nativity
  into measurable, reproducible results.
- A vocabulary partition into "cross-model stable" and
  "receiver-capability-sensitive" categories that informs what Track A
  (pedagogy) and Track B (codec) can each rely on.
- The pre-registration and verbatim-prompt practices the project still
  follows.

- Quantify which receiver-side analysis approaches reliably surface the

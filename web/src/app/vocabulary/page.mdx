# The vocabulary

These are the math-native primitives. Each one is grounded in a
mathematical relationship that the audio's structure directly expresses:
integer frequency ratios, periodic timing, continuous transformation,
discrete state sequences, co-presence, ordered time, ostensive
demonstration. The math is not labeled on top of the audio; the math
IS the audio.

Headphones recommended.

## Transformation and direction

### Becomes (continuous transformation)

A continuous change from one value to another is encoded as a smooth
pitch glide with an evolving timbre, marking the audio as a process
rather than a static relation.

<AudioPlayer
  src="/audio/v4_5/p5_becomes.wav"
  label="440 becomes 583"
  description="Continuous glide from 440 Hz to 583 Hz with timbre evolution"
/>

### Transformation (discrete step sequence)

A sequence of stable tones at different frequencies, each held for
three seconds before stepping discretely to the next. Where Becomes is
a smooth glide between two values, Transformation steps between three
or more stable states with no glide between them. The signal is a
piecewise constant function in time, and the ordered list of plateau
frequencies is the message.

<AudioPlayer
  src="/audio/v4/transformation.wav"
  label="220 to 440 to 330 to 880 Hz"
  description="Four stable plateaus, three seconds each, stepping discretely between values"
/>

### Greater (monotonic increase)

Strict directional comparison: a rising pitch motion whose endpoint
frequencies are deliberately not a clean small-integer ratio, so the
receiver cannot reduce the motion to an interval and is forced to
derive the abstract meaning "upward."

  src="/audio/v4_5/p3_greater_multi.wav"

## Composition and choice

### And (co-presence)

Multiple primitives can be combined by playing them simultaneously,
with the encoder gating the sustained component against the
pulse-train so both layers survive in the combined envelope (v4.6
fix). Mathematical conjunction expressed as acoustic superposition.

<AudioPlayer
  src="/audio/v4_5/p6_and.wav"
  label="three AND two-to-one"
  description="A 3-pulse train superposed with a 2:1 octave dyad"
/>

### Or (alternation)

A choice between alternatives is encoded as clear alternation in time:
A then B then A then B then A then B.

<AudioPlayer
  src="/audio/v4_5/p7_or.wav"
  label="two OR 3:2"
  description="Alternation between a 2-pulse count and a 3:2 dyad"
/>

### Sequence (strict temporal order)

Where And presents components in parallel and Or alternates between
them, Sequence presents components in strict left-to-right temporal
order separated by short silence gaps. The order is the meaning.

## Time and repetition

### Period (cyclic repetition)

A content played at a fixed period communicates the mathematical
relationship x(t + T) = x(t). The period is directly audible as the
time between repetitions. The encoder extends the period when the
inner content would otherwise overflow (v4.7).

  src="/audio/v4_5/p8_period.wav"

  description="A composite repeated every 0.7 seconds"
/>

## Relation by ostensive demonstration

These primitives demonstrate an abstract relation through multiple
concrete paired instances. The receiver derives the relation by
identifying what stays invariant across the pairs.

### Implies (consequence)

Functional relationships are demonstrated by paired instances where
the left side determines the right. The receiver sees the pattern
across multiple examples.

<AudioPlayer
  src="/audio/v4_5/p12_implies_multi.wav"

/>

### Function (input-output map)

A mathematical map from inputs to outputs is shown via paired
(input, output) instances. The receiver derives the function from
the examples.

<AudioPlayer

### Equal (computational equivalence)

Two expressions can be structurally different but evaluate to the same
thing. Equal is demonstrated by paired instances where the left and
right side are not the same shape but reduce to the same value across
multiple examples.

<AudioPlayer
  src="/audio/v4_5/p10_equal_multi.wav"
  label="multi-instance equality"
  description="Paired expressions that differ in form but match in value"
/>

### Negate (additive inverse, cancellation)

A content combined with its phase-inverse cancels to silence. Demonstrated
by paired instances: on the left, a content; on the right, that content
superposed with its inverse, which sums to silence. Across different
inputs the receiver sees the operator that maps "anything" to "zero."

## What this is for

These primitives are the substrate for both tracks of the project:

- **Track A** teaches them to humans as a new mathematically precise
  form of communication.
- **Track B** uses them as the building blocks of an efficient data
  codec.

The vocabulary is small on purpose. Each addition has to clear the
same bar: a receiver who shares only mathematics and acoustic physics
must be able to derive its meaning from the audio alone. New primitives
land when a piece of math we want to express is not reachable from the
current set. Most expressive range comes from composition, the way
arithmetic uses a handful of operators to reach the infinite.

The code that renders this audio is in [the public repo](https://github.com/CitadelAI-Atlas/project-continua).
